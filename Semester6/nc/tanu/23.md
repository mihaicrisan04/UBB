# Sub 23

> *Handwritten exam in blue ink.*

1. Let $f: [0, 1] \to \mathbb{R}$ be three times differentiable.

   a) (1 point) Find $a$, $b$, $c$ such that the quadrature formula

   $$\int_0^1 f(x)\,dx = a\,f(0) + b\,f(1) + c\,f'(0) + R(f)$$

   has the maximum degree of precision possible. What is that degree of precision?

   b) (1 point) Find $(Pf)(x)$, the polynomial interpolating the function $f$, given $f(0)$, $f(1)$ and $f'(0)$.

   c) (1 point) Integrate $\displaystyle\int_0^1 (Pf)(x)\,dx$ and compare the formula so obtained with the one in part a).

   d) (1 point) Find the remainder $R(f)$.

2. (3 points) Let $A = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 & 6 \\ 2 & 2 & 5 \\ 6 & 5 & 22 \end{bmatrix}$. Use Cholesky factorization to solve the system $Ax = b$, where $b = [-2\ 0\ 1]^T$.

3. (2 points) Derive a one-point iteration method that approximates the root $\sqrt{2}$, for a suitable initial value $x_0$. Discuss the order of convergence and the bound for the error.
