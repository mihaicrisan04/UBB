# Numerical Calculus Exam, 21

1) (2 points) Use Gaussian elimination with partial pivoting to solve the system

$$\begin{cases} -x_1 + 3x_2 + 2x_3 = -1 \\ 2x_1 + 4x_2 \hphantom{+ 0x_3} = 8 \\ x_1 + 2x_2 - 3x_3 = 7 \end{cases}$$

2) (2.5 points) Show that the equation $x = \dfrac{1 + \sin x}{2}$ has a unique solution $\alpha$. Then find an interval $[a, b]$ containing $\alpha$ for which the iterative method $x_{n+1} = \dfrac{1 + \sin x_n}{2}$ converges to $\alpha$, for any $x_0 \in [a, b]$. Discuss the convergence of the method, the order of convergence and give a bound for the error.

3) Let $f \in C^3[0, 1]$.

   a) (1 point) Find $(Pf)(x)$, the polynomial interpolating the function $f$, knowing $f(0)$ and $f'(1/3)$.

   b) (1 point) Integrate $\displaystyle\int_0^1 (Pf)(x)\,dx$ to obtain a quadrature formula for $\displaystyle\int_0^1 f(x)\,dx$. Find its degree of precision.

   c) (1 point) Find $A_0$, $A_1$ and $x_1$ such that the quadrature formula

   $$\int_0^1 f(x)\,dx = A_0 f(0) + A_1 f'(x_1) + R(f)$$

   has the maximum degree of precision possible. Compare the formula so obtained to the one in part b).

   d) (1.5 points) Find the remainder $R(f)$, using Peano's theorem.
