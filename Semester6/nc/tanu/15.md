# Numerical Calculus Exam, 15

> *Handwritten exam in blue ink. Some values are difficult to read.*

1) (2.5) Let $f \in C^2[-1, 1]$ twice differentiable. Derive a 1-point quadrature formula

$$\int_{-1}^{1} x^2f(x)\,dx = A_0 f(x_0) + R(f)$$

with max degree of precision. Express remainder $R(f)$ in terms of $f$.

2) $\begin{cases} 2x_1 + x_2 = -1 \\ x_1 + 2x_2 = -2 \end{cases}$

Approximate solution by third Jacobi method iteration, starting with $x^{(0)} = [0,\ 0]^T$.

3) | $x$ | 0 | 1 | 2 |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | $y$ | -2 | 1 | 3 |

Best linear polynomial approximation for this.

4) Values of $c \in \mathbb{R}$ for which the iterative method $x_{n+1} = 2(1+c)x_n - cx_n^2 - 2$ converges to $\alpha = 2$, for $x_0$ sufficiently close. What is the order of convergence? Find a bound for the error.
